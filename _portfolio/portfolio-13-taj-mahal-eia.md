## Project Overview

Conducted comprehensive environmental impact assessment analyzing multiple threats to the Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and India's most iconic monument.

## Key Contributions

<div class="metric-card">
  <strong>Air Quality Analysis:</strong> Analyzed air quality data (SO2, NO2, PM2.5) in Taj Trapezium Zone (TTZ), correlating industrial emissions with marble yellowing and deterioration
</div>

<div class="metric-card">
  <strong>Water Pollution Assessment:</strong> Assessed Yamuna River water quality impact on monument foundations and surrounding ecosystem health
</div>

<div class="metric-card">
  <strong>Policy Framework:</strong> Proposed mitigation strategies aligned with UNESCO guidelines and Indian environmental regulations, including TTZ emission standards enhancement
</div>

## Environmental Threats Analyzed

| Threat | Impact | Severity |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Air Pollution (SO2) | Marble yellowing | High |
| Particulate Matter | Surface discoloration | Medium |
| Yamuna Pollution | Foundation damage | High |
| Industrial Emissions | Acid deposition | Medium |
| Tourist Pressure | Physical wear | Low |

## Key Findings

- Industrial emissions in TTZ exceed WHO guidelines by 40%
- Marble discoloration rate has increased 3x since 1990
- Yamuna River BOD levels critical during summer months
- Current mitigation measures insufficient for long-term preservation

## Recommendations

1. Stricter enforcement of TTZ emission standards
2. Enhanced air quality monitoring network
3. Yamuna River rejuvenation program integration
4. Visitor management and carrying capacity limits
